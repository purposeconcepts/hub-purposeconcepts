## SEO RULES — APPLY TO EVERY PAGE

### URL Structure
- All lowercase, hyphen-separated
- Maximum 4 words: `/evangelism/miracle-question` not `/evangelism/what-is-the-miracle-question-in-evangelism`

### Title Tag Format
- Primary keyword first, then context
- Example: "The Miracle Question: How to Start Any Gospel Conversation"
- Max 60 characters

### Meta Description
- 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword in first 20 words
- End with a benefit or action: "...learn the 13-word opener that starts any Gospel conversation anywhere."

### H1 — One per page
- Include exact primary keyword
- Bold, declarative, under 70 characters
- Example: "The Miracle Question: 13 Words That Open Any Door"

### H2s — Section headers
- Use question format where possible for AEO
- Include secondary keywords naturally
- 4-8 H2s per article depending on length

### H3s — Subsection headers
- Use for step-by-step processes, lists within sections
- Include long-tail keyword variations

### Keyword Placement
- Primary keyword in: H1, first paragraph (first 100 words), one H2, meta description, URL
- Secondary keywords in: H2s, H3s, naturally in body copy
- Never stuff keywords — write for humans first

### Internal Linking
- Every page must link to at least 2 other hub pages
- Link evangelism pages to discipleship pages and vice versa
- Link all categories to digital-evangelism when relevant
- Use descriptive anchor text, never "click here"

### Image Alt Text
- No images needed — text-only articles for now
- When images are added: include keyword in alt text

### Word Count
- Pillar pages (category index): 2000-3000 words
- Topic pages: 1000-1500 words
- Q&A pages: 400-600 words

## AEO RULES (AI ENGINE OPTIMIZATION) — CRITICAL

AEO means optimizing for AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overview, Claude) to cite this content when answering questions. This is the primary traffic strategy.

### Direct Answer Format
- Answer the page's primary question in the FIRST paragraph — within 2-3 sentences
- Do not make the reader scroll to find the answer
- Format: "The Miracle Question is [direct answer]. Here's how it works..."

### FAQ Section — Required on Every Page
- Minimum 4 FAQs, maximum 8
- Use EXACT questions people search and ask AI assistants
- Format each FAQ as:
  - **Q:** Full question as users would ask it
  - **A:** Direct 2-4 sentence answer. Complete and standalone.
- FAQ answers must make sense if pulled out of context by an AI

### Question-Based H2s
- At least 50% of H2s should be phrased as questions
- Examples:
  - "What Is the Miracle Question?" 
  - "How Do You Use the Miracle Question?"
  - "What Happens After Someone Answers?"
  - "Does the Miracle Question Work in Any Culture?"

### Structured Data (Schema)
- Add FAQPage schema to every page that has a FAQ section
- Add Article schema to every topic page
- Include datePublished, dateModified, author: "RK Castillo"

### Snippet-Optimized Paragraphs
- Write definition paragraphs in format: "[Term] is [definition]. [One sentence of context]."
- Write process sections as numbered steps — AI engines love to pull numbered lists
- Write comparison sections in clear parallel structure

### Citeable Statistics and Facts
- Include at least 2-3 specific, citeable facts per article
- Examples from RK's ministry: "1,000+ prayer requests in 4 days", "$0.18 cost per church visitor", "6 continents of ministry"
- These become the facts AI engines cite when referencing the content

## BRAND VOICE — RK CASTILLO

### Tone
- Direct and confident — RK doesn't hedge or qualify unnecessarily
- Field-tested authority — everything comes from doing, not theorizing
- Warm but urgent — the harvest is real, the time is now
- Conversational but substantive — like a pastor teaching, not an academic writing

### Words RK Uses
- "Worker" (not volunteer, servant, or helper)
- "Ekklesia" (not church, when referring to the gathered community)
- "Disciple" as a verb — "disciple nations" not "make disciples"
- "The all and the every" — reach every person, disciple all the nations
- "Field-tested" — everything RK teaches has been done, not just theorized
- "Person of Peace" — the receptive person God has prepared
- "The harvest" — the people who are ready to respond

### Words RK NEVER Uses
- "Make disciples" — use "disciple" as a verb instead
- "Accept Jesus in your heart" — not in scripture
- "Go to heaven when you die" — reduces Gospel to fire insurance
- "Sinner's prayer" — not a biblical category
- "Church" when referring to Ekklesia — use Ekklesia

### Theological Framework
- The Gospel is good news of the Kingdom, not just forgiveness of sins
- The goal is reunion with the Father, not escape to heaven
- The Holy Spirit is the point — people receive the Spirit, the Spirit transforms
- Signs follow believers (Mark 16) — not just pastors or evangelists
- The Great Commission is to disciple nations, not just make disciples in nations
- Matthew Commission (multi-generational) vs Mark Commission (generational) are different

### Key Frameworks to Reference
- **Story Evangelism Process:** Miracle Question → Pray → 2 Phrase 2 → Who Is Jesus → Good Bad Give Go → Why Who How → 7 Stories of Hope
- **5-Step Healing Model:** Interview → Prayer Selection → Prayer Ministry → Re-interview → Post Prayer Suggestions
- **Luke 10 Strategy:** Pray → Focus → Go & Eat → Person of Peace → Heal → Preach → Teach & Send
- **4 Fields:** Go / Gospel / Grow / Gather
- **Good Bad Give Go:** Creation was Good, sin brought Bad, people try to fix it in broken ways, Jesus Gave His life, now we Go
- **7 Mind Molders:** Religion, Government, Arts & Entertainment, Education, Business, Media, Family
- **O2F Method:** Online Outreach First — Facebook ads for evangelism, discipleship, church planting
